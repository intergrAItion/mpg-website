## The Industry Standard Fee in South Africa

Most property management companies in South Africa charge between 10 and 12 percent of monthly rental income as their management fee. On a property renting for R15,000 per month, that is R1,500 to R1,800 disappearing from your income every single month, before you have even paid for maintenance, levies, or rates. Over a year, that adds up to between R18,000 and R21,600 per property.

The question worth asking is not just what you are paying, but what you are getting for it.

## What Should Be Included for That Fee

A fair management fee at the 10 to 12 percent mark should cover a clear set of services. At a minimum, you should expect your agent to handle tenant sourcing, screening, and placement; draft and manage lease agreements in line with the Rental Housing Act; collect rent and follow up on arrears proactively; coordinate maintenance requests with qualified contractors; conduct move-in and move-out inspections with formal documentation; and provide monthly financial statements so you can track your income and expenses clearly.

If any of those services are billed separately, or if you are paying extra for basic compliance work, you are likely overpaying.

## Common Ways Landlords Get Overcharged

The most frequent issue is fee creep. An agent may quote a competitive headline rate, then add charges for lease renewals, inspection reports, maintenance call-outs, and administrative tasks that should already be covered. Over twelve months, these extras can add several thousand rands to your annual cost.

A second issue is poor arrears management. If your agent is slow to follow up on late payments, you lose income directly. Effective rent collection should be a core part of any management fee, not an optional extra.

Finally, vague or infrequent reporting leaves many landlords in the dark about what is actually happening with their property. If you cannot easily track what rent was collected, what was deducted, and why, that is a red flag.

## Practical Steps to Evaluate and Reduce Costs

Start by requesting a full breakdown of every fee and charge you are currently paying. Compare this against what your agreement says is included. Ask your current agent directly what is covered in the management fee and what is billed additionally.

Next, benchmark your rate. Management fees in the 7 to 8 percent range are achievable without sacrificing service quality, particularly for landlords with multiple properties or properties in good condition.

Finally, look at the quality of reporting you receive. Monthly statements should be clear, itemised, and easy to read. If they are not, consider whether your agent is providing the transparency your investment deserves.
